If you all are still with me after my very lengthy debut post, then kudos to you. It seems that you have taken an interest in my twisted and confusing journey. I will say that this article will be much more compact than the last one. And frankly, it will be much more useful to you all. Not just the CS students looking to nab that sweet internship, but frankly, anybody. Please realize that any path that you take is a marathon, not a race. It has taken me YEARS to realize that. I would give up because I would not obtain instantaneous results. But your ability to reach your goals will only be dependent on your grit, and determination.


Now after reading up on my mistakes in the past 4 years that resulted me in wasting my education, you all are probably wondering what I am going to do. I need a job fast. I will be set to graduate in December. That is not a lot of time. So here is what I am going to do. I will be restarting my tech journey. Yes, you heard that right. Back to zero. All the way back to the basics. You are probably thinking that I am crazy, and that I do not need to go all the way back. But you need to understand that my knowledge is the equivalent of a puddle. I need to get it to the Pacific Ocean. And how will that work? By re-establishing my fundamental understanding of technology and building on top of it.

Here is the roadmap I am following to re-establish my understanding of technology. I am following this amazing article by CodeBasics. Please Give him a follow. However, I am making some key adjustments.


Computer Science Extreme Basics.

Square One. That is what I am going back towards. Gosh I feel like a 14 year old again, taking my very first programming class. But yes, I will be going all the way back to how a computer works. Do you know that I do not even know how to convert numbers to binary? Its very embarrasing. So this will be my first foundational building block. Here are the steps I am taking to complete this module.

  • Digital Information Storage - How does a computer store information, binary calculations
  • How the internet Works - HTTP protocols, sending requests, what is returned from those requests
  • What is Programming? - Definition of programming, and components of a computer program
  • Basics of Algorithms - What is an algorithm, why is efficiency important
  • Object Oriented - Classes, Objects, Encapsulation, Polymorphism

Front End Development

Still in basics. I feel like once I understand standard computer functionality, I can start some basic development. Which in this case is the standard HTML & CSS. I have purchased 2 online courses in the past that I will review. But instead of skipping around, I will actually digest all the information, and do as many projects I can.

High Level Programming Language

This will get a bit more challenging. But I will thoroughly learn, and apply one high level programming language. I was 50/50 on javascript or python, but given how versatile it is, and the number of jobs in full stack development in this language, I think I will stick with Javascript.

Data Structures

After being proficient in the high level programming language, I will familiarize myself with every data structure that can be used. Even though I did say I will stick with Javascript for my high level language, I think I will implement using Python. I am still not sure though. But here is the list of Data Structures I will familiarize myself with.

  • Array
  • Stacks
  • Queues
  • Linked List
  • Hashmap
  • Trees

Databases

Again, I will re-evaluate my SQL skills as I have experience. But I will thoroughly go through every concept, as well as methods to scale databases. I will also go over NoSQL databases and the essential differences between SQL and NoSQL.

Algorithms

Finally, I will dive deep into algorithms. I will cover every common algorithm used in interviews, and learn how to implement them. I have done a few DS&A interviews and failed every single one of them. I do not want this to keep recurring. I want to thoroghly understand the importance of every algorithm, and be able to calculate the space and time complexity. Here is the list of Algorithms I will cover.

  • Algorithm Analysis - Space and Time Complexity
  • Binary Search
  • Sorting - Bubble, Quick, Insertion, Merge, Selection, Shell
  • Recursion
  • Linked List algorithms
  • Tree Traversal - BFS, DFS, Preorder, Postorder, Inorder
  • Dijiktra's Algorithm
